Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How do I start a terrace garden as a complete beginner?

To start a terrace garden: first, choose containers with proper drainage holes. Second, prepare a lightweight, nutrient-rich potting soil mix (equal parts garden soil, compost, and cocopeat). Third, start with easy-to-grow plants like spinach, green chillies, mint, or marigolds. Place them where they receive at least 4 to 6 hours of direct sunlight daily.

What is the perfect soil recipe for container gardening?

The ideal potting mix for terrace container gardening is: 30% Organic Compost (vermicompost or cow manure) for nutrients, 30% Cocopeat for water retention and aerated structure, 30% Red/Garden Soil for mineral support, and 10% Neem Cake Powder to prevent soil-borne pests and root rots.

How often should I water my rooftop potted plants in summer?

In hot summer months, water your rooftop plants twice a day—early in the morning before 8 AM and late in the evening after 6 PM. Always check the top 1-inch of the soil: if it feels dry to the touch, water the plant thoroughly until water drains out from the bottom holes. Avoid watering in direct afternoon heat to prevent root shock.

How can I control pests on my terrace garden organically?

For organic pest control: spray a neem oil solution (mix 5ml neem oil + 2-3 drops of liquid dish soap in 1 liter of warm water) every 10-14 days. You can also spray dilute wood ash for powdery mildew, use sticky yellow traps for whiteflies, and plant companion herbs like basil and marigold to naturally repel insects.

Do potted plants damage the rooftop slab or cause leakages?

No, plants do not damage the roof if set up correctly. Use raised drainage stands or metal/wooden plant racks to raise pots off the slab. This ensures water drains freely and allows the roof surface to dry. You can also apply a professional waterproofing paint or layer a tarpaulin sheet on the terrace floor before placing containers.
